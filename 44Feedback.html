POLYISO: INCREASE THE INSULATION IN OUR WINDOWS

Feedback from Stakeholders

Jeff Alger, Carpenter Shop for Dartmouth Facilities, Operations, and Management
Pointed out the potential difficulties in changing window structure
  • Windows are supported by wooden studs on the two sides and top. Must work around them
  • Window width. Currently, dual-paned glass requires a space of 1 1/4 inches. If we are to add any type of insulating panels, we have to widen the width of the window frame.

Bruce Plann, Design Consultant at Oakes Marvin Showcase Dealer in West Lebanon, NH
Positives and negatives of current window technology
  • Triple-pane windows are too heavy to be worth the additional 2.3 R-value
  • Blinds decline over time and need to be replaced
  • Current research is focused on developing more effective seals to prevent moisture from entering dual-pane glass
Bottom line: Consumers are extremely adamant about clear and clean glass. "I want excellent clarity, and if it gets dirty, I want it to be easy to clean."
