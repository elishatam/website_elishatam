The HC Controller



1.) Boat Power Board

Two 7.2V batteries are placed in parallel. To obtain 5V, we run the 7.2V through a voltage regulator. The data sheet indicates that the voltage regulator needs a minimum dropout voltage of 2V. When fully charged, the batteries have an average voltage of 8.3V and our circuit runs smoothly. However, we may encounter problems when our batteries drain out and dip below 7V.

After the voltage regulator, we tap from these 5V's to power the other circuit boards. A power MOSFET protects the components in case the batteries are plugged in backwards. The kill switch opens and closes the connection.




2.) Boat Main Board

The main board contains 3 PICS (MAIN, RFID, and AUXILIARY PICS) as well as the Security Controller and an Inverter.


MAIN PIC

The Main PIC handles all of the XB communication with the controller, neptune, and broadcasts. It also handles SPI communication with the RFID PIC and the AUX PIC.

3.) HC E128 Board

The E128 controls the logic for the HC controller. The LCD, potentiometer, accelerometer, and microphone circuits are controlled by the E128.
