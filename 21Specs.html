The specifications that were derived from the consumer group through surveying are functionality, reliability, and safety.

Functionality is a characteristic that refers to the product not interfering with the normal operation of existing hardware that it interfaces with, e.g. the stove and the cell-phone. In the case of the stove-top notification system, functionality will be achieved if the product does not interfere with the normal operation of the user’s stovetop and cell.

Reliability refers to failure over the lifespan of the product.

Safety refers to the product not creating additional hazards to the user that are not already present in the operation of a stovetop and cell phone when the product is operated by the user in the manner intended and for the purpose intended by the manufacturer.
