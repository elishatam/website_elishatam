POLYISO: INCREASE THE INSULATION IN OUR WINDOWS

Problem Definition
  • Windows account for at least 1/3 of the heat loss from buildings
    (http://energyhawk.com/heat/heat3.php)
  • We seek to decrease the amount of energy required to heat buildings and its associated monetary and environmental costs
  • We wish to increase the insulating properties of windows without limiting their solar collection capabilities

Analysis of Need / Opportunity
  • Heating residential and commercial buildings is the single greatest heating requirement. In the U.S. this amounts to an average of 1/5 of the total U.S. energy consumption. 42% of this consumed energy is lost simply through heat
    (http://www.wbdg.org/design/minimize_consumption.php)
  • The U.S. uses 97.4 quadrillion BTUs of energy = ¼ of the world’s energy resources. The U.S. population only accounts for 5% of the world population
    (http://www.nef1.org/ea/eastats.html)

Ideal User Profile
  • Use in commercial and industrial buildings with large banks of standardized windows
  • Implmented as a prefabricated wall panel with integrated window and apparatus
  • Automated solution to operate all the windows
  • Geography: New England, Upper Midwest, Rocky Mountains. Locations with a significant temperature difference between day and night to make the most out of our solution
  • Market our innovation to window manufacturers (i.e. Pella or Andersen). In this context, our product would be incorporated into their existing window designs.
